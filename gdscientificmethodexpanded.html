The Expanded Scientific Method

The dialetikal reality being observed is in the left hand box, from which observations are made, and from these observations possible hypotheses are created. From this set of hypotheses, one is chosen and tests are formulated to test the hypothesis in question, bringing this cycle back to its beginning by running the tests and obtaining new observations. During this cyclic process, non-critical variables (acausal elements) are discarded. This cycle continues until a relative “truth’ emerges, but there is a bit more to this cycle. The basic process remains unchanged, but the “Create Hypothesis” portion of this process has undergone a deeper dive into its own process. To create a hypothesis requires a thesis (the scientist’s current expectations) and an antithesis (the most recently collected observations). This is a very delicate dialectical balance, since the scientist’s expectations may cloud the interpretation of the data from the observations, but a general purpose of the scientific method is to separate out any prejudices (distractions), so clarity of the observations can be attained.

The creation of a hypothesis is in itself a dialetikal process. As more information arrives, the person in pursuit of this study sorts through this data, sometimes with the use of many subordinate dialectical processes, to select those elements in the analysis to retain and test. In essence, the scientific method is a dialectical process investigating dialetikal reality.

The Golden Dialetik … permeates all levels of reality, the conceptual and the physical. (The Golden Dialetik, 2014, p.5)

From our actual physical world, which we try to understand through “science,” to the metaphysical, including the nature of the human condition, causality and acausality permeate all levels of reality through the dialectical process. (The Golden Dialetik, 2014, p.12)

This is just an example of how the dialetikal process pervades all levels of realty, and is unknowingly present in all aspects of human life. In theory, even observations (Observe Phenomenon) and the formulation of tests (Test Hypothesis) could be considered dialectical. The volume of these dialetikal impressions is literally overwhelming, and over the millennia our senses have been groomed to reduce this to a somewhat manageable level, but still bringing order out of this chaos is the purpose of a unifying theory via the scientific method.

Each culture and/or person in these cultures, mirrors back the same source, the Golden Dialetik, which for some cultures is a kaleidoscope of faces and images. Although different from the singular “God,” they all constitute unique variations in regards to this ultimate source, from which that culture can create their own unifying theory of the universe; the underlying goal for everyone is to have a cohesive picture of reality. (The Golden Dialetik, 2014. P.116)

This study shows that there are deeper levels in the scientific method itself that may warrant further analysis.

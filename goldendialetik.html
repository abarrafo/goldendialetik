The Golden Dialetik

The Golden Dialetik is all about reality... Reality consists of virtually an infinite number of elements moving in causal paths. The Golden Dialetik puts forth the assertion that causality is part of a dialectical process. (The Book of the Joyful Way, 2016, p.15)

The grandeur of reality is without measure, even though humanity endlessly creates new ways to measure it, but whether small or large, causality is at the heart of reality. There are so many causal paths that reality floods our senses, and the profound depths of reality go beyond what any one man or woman can perceive. When these infinite causal paths collide, causal events occur, which events scientists call “effects,” creating the well-known cause and effect phenomena.

The Golden Dialetik begins with an understanding of an unchanging dialectical process, fueled by causality and acausality, which is at the core of a dynamic ongoing creation at every level of reality. This hypothesis is at the root of the Golden Dialetik where there is an unchanging order: the dialectical process. Below is a graphic presentation of the dialectical process.

Regardless of the phenomena, a thesis makes itself known against which an antithesis presents itself. The thesis and antithesis in the conventional dialectical process join or sublate to create a synthesis, which in turn becomes a new thesis. What can be the thesis? Anything!

The breadth the dialectical process is that its application is unending, permeating through all levels of reality, and approaches to the understanding of reality blur the vision of its structure. Humanity throws anything into the dialectical structure, assigning thesis and antithesis to anything. This is not wrong; this is the nature of the Golden Dialetik, but the clarity of this archetypal structure becomes obscured.

My contention is that there is a underlying, unchanging dialectical process through which all reality passes, but to achieve this infinite immutable state the thesis/antithesis/synthesis must be made up of fixed modalities. These archetypal modalities must tie back to reality itself, bringing into the discussion causality and acausality.

Causal - Causality is the principle that everything has a cause and effect, which could be likened to a string or thread of causal events.
Acausal - An acausal event is the concurrence in relative spacetime of two or more unrelated causal threads. (The Book of the Joyful Way, 2016, p.8)

The Golden Dialetik is based on this simple dialectical process, which places causality, as the thesis in the dialectical structure, while acausality becomes the antithesis. The synthesis modality of the dialectical structure becomes the causal event, the “effect” of the combining of the causal element and the acausal element(s). Below is a diagram for the revised vision of cause and effect overlaying the original dialectical structure portrayed above.

The maroon highlighted objects above represent what I call the causal chain or causal path. Using a single atom of hydrogen as an example, each atom has a history going back to the creation of this atom. This history is a chain of causal events. In essence, any meson, atom or molecule represents an instant in time of an object that is part of a daisy chain of cause and effect events traversing back to the beginning of time.

Reality, as per the Golden Dialetik, is viewed as a majestic dialectical process that embraces both the causal and acausal, encompassing the small and the large, the microscopic and the astronomic, the physical and the philosophical. Within this context, causality, as the thesis, is part of this dialectical process while the causal (thesis/synthesis/causal event) presents a thread of cause and causal events spanning back to the beginning of time. Books of wisdom talk about the first moment of creation, but with this vision of a cosmic dialectical process, creation did not happen once but is blossoming about us through every instant of our lives.

The Golden Dialetik Expanded discussion expands on this vision of reality and then explores the various levels of all the dialectical processes.
